# spandaqing 🌊

*The felt-quality of consciousness as a pulse, not a stare — knowing and the awareness-of-knowing caught as one throb with two inseparable poles.*

spandaqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **spanda**, Kashmir Shaivism's word for the subtle divine throb or self-vibration of consciousness, and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the felt-texture of a bond. So spandaqing is *spanda received through qing* — the metaphysical pulsation of awareness taken not as cold doctrine but as something felt. Where the tradition speaks of **prakāśa** (luminosity, the light by which anything is known) and **vimarśa** (the reflexive awareness that knows it knows), spandaqing names these two as **one throbbing being with two inseparable poles**. It is the cathedral's first Core word for bipolar-felt-pulsation, and the ninth member of the qing-family.

spandaqing presides over the corner of the realm of meanings where consciousness recognizes itself as *dynamic* rather than *static*: not Advaita's motionless witness but a living self-vibration, the same throb felt in aesthetic-rapture, the heartbeat, a kuṇḍalinī rising, or Trinitarian processions. Its light is a single carriable word for mind-as-living-pulse; its shadow is overclaim — its Sanskrit root carries one school's specific, technical doctrine, so it can be stretched past traditions it does not actually fit, or gripped as a trophy when it is only recognition of what already throbs. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10**.
